SUMMARY

The question of the second Cainan begins with Luke 3:36 but clearly has wider implications. Although it is extremely difficult to be dogmatic in this matter, the most likely sequence of events is that:

(1)      Luke personally included the second Cainan in accordance with the LXX and tradition (Jubilees)

(2)     Both the pre-Christian and Christian copies of the LXX did include the second Cainan

(3)      The second Cainan was omitted by the Jews because of being in the line of Shem and yet cursed[2907]

(4)     The patriarchal genealogy of the LXX is overall more consistent than that of MT and ST.

This approach is of course in harmony with the Orthodox view that the LXX is a very reliable witness to the Old Testament and the text of reference in the New Testament.[2908] Even though due consideration is given to the MT and other Hebrew platforms, the Orthodox approach is that the LXX remains normative when the evidence is inconclusive.
