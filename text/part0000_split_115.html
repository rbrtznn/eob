ECCLESIATICAL USE

Besides all the data from patristic quotations and Greek MSS, there is also the evidence from the lectionaries, books containing a long series of selections from Scripture to read during church services. There are hundreds of lectionaries in existence, and lectionary related rubrics appear in the margins of hundreds of manuscripts. Mark 16:9-20 was (and remains to this day in Orthodox practice) a reading selection for the Feast of Ascension, and it was the second of eleven special selections called the Eothina, about the rising of Jesus.

This further confirms the widespread recognition of Mark 16:9-20 as a fully (albeit possibly non-Markan) received ecclesiastical witness.
