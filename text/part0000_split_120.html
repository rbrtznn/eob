UNDERSTANDING THE PATTERNS

Why did the Masoretes apply a formula of 100 year reduction in six names, and not apply the same formula to Jared, Methuselah and Lamech? One critical reason may be offered: if the Masoretes had changed Jared, Methuselah and Lamech in line with the previous six names, this would have brought the year of the flood down to 1356 AM (Anno Mundi), and each of these three patriarchs would have lived past the Flood. The formula had to be ignored for Jared, Methuselah and Lamech to avoid contradicting the date of the Flood.

This difference in Masoretic ages - down and then up before the flood - created a dysfunction in the pattern of ages which has been noted by chronographers throughout history.

Column 5 shows Masoretic Lamech’s age at his death to be 1651, whereas the Flood is shown to have occurred in the year 1656, five years after. Again, if Lamech’s age was not reduced by six years, Lamech would have been found to have lived past the Flood by one year, which would explain why his age had to be changed to fit in with the Flood formula. This could be interpreted as a deliberate alteration on the part of these Masoretic scribes.

If this is the case, it may be further suggested that these Masoretic variations were deliberate alterations made to adhere to the new ages of the world as prescribed in the Sedar Olam Rabba (Great Chronicle of the World), a treatise written by the Pharisees.
